A list of network and syndicated shows that aired on KPTV during its first 50 years.  These are programs that were supplied to stations either by their affiliated network, or by a non-affiliated producer.  The codes below indicate the program's origination at the time it aired on KPTV. (1964+)
[ABC] Shows that aired on KPTV directly from the ABC Network.
[CBS] Shows that aired on KPTV directly from the CBS Network.
[FOX] Shows that aired on KPTV directly from the Fox Network.
[NBC] Shows that aired on KPTV directly from the NBC Network.
[UPN] Shows that aired on KPTV directly from the UPN Network
[ONR]  Off-network reruns: Programs that aired in first-run on a broadcast network, then were aired in reruns on KPTV.
[SYN]  First-run syndication: Programs that aired for the first time on KPTV and other local stations outside of a network. 
[SYR] Syndicated reruns: Programs that originally aired in syndication, then aired in reruns on KPTV.

 A series developed by Jack Webb ("Dragnet") about two Los Angeles police officers, and the calls they responded to in a normal work day. Like "Dragnet," this show stressed authenticity, as the officers responded to several calls in each episode. Martin Milner and Kent McCord starred. The series aired on NBC from 1968-75. GAMBIT - Game Show
Two husband-and-wife couples competed against each other by answering questions. A right answer gave them the option to keep or to give away a playing card in a game of blackjack. The object was to score "21" exactly, or get as close as possible to 21 without going over before the opponent did. The first team to win two games won the match, $200, and a chance to play the Gambit Board for merchandise prizes. Wink Martindale was the host. The 1978 episodes were repeats of the 1972-76 CBS daytime series.

Although the series never garnered high ratings during it's years on NBC (1966-69), the show achieved cult status when it began reruns on local stations around the country, in 1969.  The series became a KPTV staple, airing for more than 30 years. It finally left the KPTV airwaves after Paramount, the show's syndicator, sold the exclusive rights to cable's Sci-Fi Channel.
