Wolfard's Supper Club
April 9, 1957 - December 12, 1957+

 

Gene Brendler hosted this live half-hour of humor and the musical interpretations of Ginny Becker, Jack Howell, Joe Ingram, Bob Anson and Al Barker. Red hot jazz and special guests were the main entrees for this program, which was broadcast from the KPTV studios.


From the "Hurd This Week" column in TV Prevue Magazine
May 5, 1957

Local show, Wolfard's Supper Club, Channel 27, has changed time to 9:30 p.m. Tuesdays. Coupla weeks back show went on the air with host Gene Brendler running (live) down a street with two Mack Sennett type cops chasing him. By the time the show actually got on the air and past the very sneaky credits, Brendler was so exhausted and out of breath he could hardly talk. Ole Fatso should get rid of some of that lard around his middle. About midway through the show Gene did a commercial that started out with a soft shoe dance wherein Brendler nearly fell on his face, which if he had would indeed have been good for a laugh. All in all it's a good, very good show with Brendler doing a Steve Allen, just as good as Allen himself. Ginny Becker is cute and sings up a storm. Music not bad, which should make this show a ROSIE contender for '57. If you don't watch it you're a coward.


Ginny Becker and Gene Brendler
