The Next Half Hour
February 1956 - April 1956+ (scheduled monthly)

Produced in KPTV's studios, this program was a standout effort to have locally-produced dramas featuring local actors and playwrights. Airing irregularly, the casts often included KPTV personnel such as Gene Brendler and Gordon White.

Some listings of the shows produced:

Local Live Drama:  "Black Orange Blossoms" will be presented at 8:30pm this Sunday as the second show in ch. 27's new monthly series, The Next Half Hour.  Scripts are by Vn Stuart of Portland, who has written for sucah network programs as the The Web and Danger.  Von, who has appeared on TV and the Broadway stage is continuing to write for network shows while living in Portland.  Casts are composed of local talent.  TV GUIDE, March 24, 1956.

3/25/56 8:30pm: "Black Orange Blossoms."  A notorious gunman and his blond girl friend seek refuge in a deserted chapel, along with another couple.  A conflict arises between the gunman and the Mother Superior.

4/15/56 8:30pm: rescheduled for 4/15/56. Glenna Page, Rick Dorio, star.
