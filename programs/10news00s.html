The 10 O'Clock News 2000-2002


2000: One of many different opens to the show featured KPTV's
lovely Willamette River office building.


Kim Singer is joined by Kurt Ludlow, who took over anchor
duties in 1999.


Tim Becker continues as sports anchor.


New computerized graphics, which allowed the viewer to "fly
over" the terrain arrived, as did weathercaster Rod Hill.


Kim Singer with a snazzy "Campaign 2000" graphic.


Kurt Ludlow reports on the rising gas prices.


February 29, 2000: After 16 years anchoring The Ten O'Clock
News, Kim Singer signs off. Kim is given moral support by
Rod Hill, weather; Kurt Ludlow, anchor; and Tim Becker, sports.


A long shot of the studio on that same evening shows the
news desk on the swivel platform, which could rotate 360
degrees. Cameraman Mark Mack captures Rod Hill, Kim Singer,
Kurt Ludlow and Tim Becker as the show ends.


 The 10 O'Clock News opening titles, 2001.


The 10 O'Clock News team, October 2000 to 2002:
Rod Hill (weather), Kurt Ludlow (anchor),
Shauna Parsons (anchor), Tim Becker (sports). 


Kurt and Shauna open the news from studio "A".


Rod Hill recaps the days weather from the "weatherdeck," the
third story balcony overlooking the Willamette River at KPTV's
east-bank facility.
