Kimberly Maus

Good Day Oregon’s co-host, Kimberly Maus, grew up as a middle child with an older brother and a younger brother in Palm Springs, California. This undoubtedly provided early training for dealing with the antics of co-host Ken Ackerman, Traffic Reporter Tony Martinez, and Weather Guy Andy Carson every weekday morning from 7-9am on Oregon’s 12.

After graduating from Arizona State in 1989, Kimberly began cutting her broadcasting teeth as a radio reporter the following year. She soon found herself back in her home town of Palm Springs anchoring the 5 and 6pm newscasts for KESQ-TV.

In 1993 Kimberly moved north to San Luis Obispo and anchored KSBY’s 11pm newscast for five years.

After an exhaustive talent search, the management team for Oregon’s News 12 tabbed Kimberly as the new co-host for Good Day Oregon—an exciting and upbeat two hours of local news programming that has seen substantial ratings success since its inception in the Fall of 1996.

Kimberly loves the outdoors. She looks forward to exploring Oregon and you can expect to see her pursuing her interests in rock climbing, water skiing, horseback riding, and even country line dancing on future editions of Good Day Oregon.

KPTV Press Release, 1999
