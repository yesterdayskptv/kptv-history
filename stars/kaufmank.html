Kasey Kaufman is a familiar face to Boston television viewers since joining WBZ-TV 4 in January 1986 as a news anchor and reporter. An Emmy Award-winning journalist, Kaufman anchored the station’s weekend evening newscasts for 15 years, in addition to reporting for WBZ 4 News weekdays. She now handles an expanded weekday reporting role and responsibilities as a primary fill-in anchor for the station’s weekday newscasts. During her tenure with WBZ 4 News, Kaufman has covered every type of major news event including several presidential elections to, most recently, reporting from Ground Zero in the aftermath of the September 11th terror attack on America.

Before joining WBZ-TV 4 Kaufman was a news anchor and correspondent at WCAU-TV in Philadelphia, a CBS owned and operated TV station. She was recognized for her work with the prestigious Edward R. Murrow Award for the station’s team coverage of the MOVE bombing. Since then she has been nominated for several Emmy Awards and received the American Women in Radio and TV award in 1989.

Kaufman began her broadcasting career in 1980 in Burlington, Vermont, where she served as News Director and Anchor at WVNY-TV, an ABC affiliated station. She then moved on to Portland, Oregon where she was the weeknight News Anchor at KPTV.

In addition to her on-air work, Kaufman is involved in a number of charitable organizations. She is the co-founder of the Max Warburg Courage Curriculum, a literacy program in the Boston Public Schools. She also serves on the board of the Women’s Cancer Center at Massachusetts General Hospital. In addition, she has been active in Family’s First, The Women’s Lunch Place. In 1994 she also taught a journalism class to undergraduates at the Kennedy School at Harvard.

Kaufman graduated with honors from the University of California in Los Angeles and attended Pembroke College at Cambridge University.

Kaufman lives in Chestnut Hill with her husband and 2 children.
