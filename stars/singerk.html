Kim Singer

Kim Singer began her Portland broadcasting career as a reporter/trainee at KATU in 1975.  She quickly assumed the responsibilities of general assignment reporter and consumer reporter with a regular segment on money management. She also co-hosted the "Sunday Morning" talk show with Rick Meyers at KATU.

In 1978, Kim moved to KABC in Los Angeles as general assignment reporter with an emphasis on the entertainment industry.

In 1979 she began working at KHJ, the RKO-owned radio station in Los Angeles.  While there, one of her documentaries on the California prison system won her an Emmy Award.

Kim took on a different aspect of broadcasting when, in 1981, she became co-owner of KCRF radio in Lincoln City, Oregon.  Starting the station from the ground up, Kim wore many hats, including advertising sales and news reporting.

In February of 1984, Kim joined the staff of KPTV, Oregon's 12 and has remained a fixture at the news anchor position ever since.  Her straightforward approach to reporting on The 10 O'Clock News has made her a favorite in the Portland market for more than a decade.

Kim likes to spend time with her many pets and also enjoys skiing and golf.
