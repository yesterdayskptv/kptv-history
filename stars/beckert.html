Tim Becker

Tim Becker started anchoring sports at Oregon’s 12 in January of 1997.

Television careers run through in Tim’s family like the Blazers on a fast-break. Tim has a brother who is a news reporter in Charlotte, NC, a sister-in-law who is an anchor also in Charlotte, another sister-in-law is a documentary producer in San Francisco!

Born in Asia, and raised there until the age of 12 when his family moved to East Wenatchee, WA, Tim eventually attended Pacific Lutheran University.

An avid skier, Tim’s career has almost always kept him near the mountains. He anchored sportscasts at KRDV in Medford for five years before taking a job in Florida…but Florida’s lack of ski slopes eventually had Tim looking back to the Northwest. Salt Lake City brought the promise of untracked powder on the slopes. Tim took a sabbatical from television, and drove a ski-bus for a short time. The best part of the job, according to Tim, was the free ski-pass. In July of 1994, Tim took a job with KOMO in Seattle, just as the roofing tiles in the Kingdome started to fall, and the Seahawks appeared to be moving to Los Angeles.

"It seems like covering sports up in Seattle was more about politics and everything else but the games," remarks Tim. "It’ll be nice to be working in a city where by-in-large the teams will be sticking around for a while."

Tim won a national IRIS award nomination for "Revenge of the Siskiyous," a half-hour program covering a mountain bike race in Southern Oregon, an Oregon High School Coaches Association Prep Sports Media Person of the Year award, and several Associated Press awards for sports reporting.
