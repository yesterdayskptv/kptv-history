Rod Hill

For some, giving is the most important part of the day. Such is the case with Rod Hill, who can be seen giving reliable weather reports every weeknight on The 10 O’Clock News. When he’s not on the Oregon’s 12 Weather Deck, Rod is using his certificates from both the American Meteorological Society and the National Weather Association to give area students the basics of weather science and how to predict it. Rod came to Oregon’s 12 in the spring of 1999 after spending four years as staff meteorologist at WCPO in Cincinnati. He has his Bachelor of Arts degree in TV/Film Communications from the University of Missouri and a certificate in Broadcast Meteorology from Mississippi State University. In 1999, The National Association of Television Arts and Sciences honored him with the Emmy award for "Best Weathercaster" in the Northwest, and the Associated Press named him "Best Weathercaster" in 2000.

Rod gives whatever is left of his free time to his two dogs, a Labrador and a Chow.

 

Rod Hill's career has taken him from the hurricane seasons of south Texas to the snows of Michigan, the tornado season of the Ohio valley and for the last four years the hard to predict weather of the Pacific Northwest.

 

Growing up in Missouri, Rod holds a Bachelors degree in Radio/Television Speech Communications from the University of Missouri. While forecasting in Texas, Rod earned his Certificate of Broadcast Meteorology from Mississippi State University and later was certified by both the American Meteorological Society and the National Weather Association. For the past two years Rod has served on the NWA's Broadcast Certification Committee.

 

Since moving to Portland, Rod has received four consecutive Emmy nominations and was awarded the prestigious Emmy award for best Meteorologist in 1999 and 2002. In 2000, Rod was honored by the Associated Press, being named best Meteorologist in the state of Oregon.

 

When he is not keeping an eye on the sky, Rod enjoys time at home with his two dogs, Caruso and Concerto. The other joys in his life are golf and ballroom dancing. Rod has won numerous awards on the dance floor and travels the country competing in anything from Foxtrot to a little Cha Cha Cha.
