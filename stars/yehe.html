EMERALD YEH is KRON's “Midday” anchor weekdays at 11:30 a.m. and consumer reporter of Contact 4 which airs on “KRON 4 News” at 5 p.m. and 6 p.m. and on “Contact 4 on BayTV”. She joined KRON in September 1984 after working as news anchor at Cable News Network in Atlanta, Georgia. Before CNN, Emerald worked as weeknight anchor and co-producer of the news program at KPTV, an independent TV station in Portland, Oregon, and as reporter and anchor at KITV, the ABC affiliate in Honolulu.

Emerald is a graduate of the University of Hawaii where she majored in journalism and minored in political science. She got a Master's degree in journalism from Columbia University in 1980.

Since joining KRON, Emerald has won numerous broadcast journalism awards, including six Emmys from the Northern California chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Science. Emerald is also involved in many community organizations. She was a board member for the United Way and the Center for the Pacific Rim. She is now a board member for the Asian Pacific Fund.

Born in Princeton, New Jersey, Emerald Yeh grew up in Hong Kong, Tokyo and Macao before her family eventually settled in Honolulu.  She has also lived in Georgia, Oregon and New York.  Reflecting on her worldwide travels, Yeh says she feels most at home in San Francisco.

Yeh anchors KRON 4 News Midday weekdays at 11:30 a.m. and is a Contact 4 reporter.  Her Contact 4 reports, which examine consumer issues and provide viewers with problem-solving information, air on KRON 4 News at 5 and 6 p.m.  Yeh joined KRON 4 in September 1984 as weekend co-anchor and weekday general assignment reporter.  Her evolving duties included co-anchoring KRON 4 News at 5 and KRON 4 News Nightbeat at 11:00 p.m.  She became anchor of Midday in 1994, when she also began her consumer reporting.

Yeh came to KRON 4 after a year and a half at CNN in Atlanta, Georgia.  At CNN, she started out as an overnight anchor, but within a month was promoted to anchor of Daybreak, CNN’s live three-hour early morning newscast.

Before CNN, Yeh worked as a weeknight anchor and co-producer of the news program at KPTV, an independent TV station in Portland, Oregon, and as a reporter and anchor at KITV, the ABC affiliate in Honolulu.

Since joining KRON 4, Yeh has won numerous broadcast journalism awards, including six Emmy Awards from the Northern California Chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences.  In 1986, Yeh won an Emmy for the report “Grandparents’ Rights.”  She received another Emmy in 1987 for her sports story, “Skipper Lynn.”  She won a Best News Feature Emmy in 1989 for “David Laird: Transplant Pioneer.” 

Yeh was awarded her fourth Emmy in 1990, for the documentary “Children of Courage.” Her “Rape of Nanking” report earned a fifth Emmy in 1999, and her sixth Emmy was for contributing to a ten-part series on HMOs.

Other broadcasting awards include three honors from the Radio-Television News Directors Association (RTNDA): Best Feature in 1986 for “Summer Camp: Children of Alcoholics,” Best 30-Minute Newscast in 1990 for KRON 4 News at 5, and Best Feature in 1993 for “Holocaust Heroes.”  The “Summer Camp: Children of Alcoholics” report also won United Press International’s national Best Feature award.  And in 2001, Yeh won an Associated Press Television Radio Association award for Best Series for “Sudden Wealth: Want to Be a Millionaire?”

In 1999, the Consumer Action organization presented Yeh and her Contact 4 colleagues with its Consumer Excellence award.  That same year, the League of Women Voters of San Francisco bestowed its “Women Who Could Be President” award upon Yeh.  The American Psychiatric Association honored her documentary, “Kids First: A Cry for Help” in 1993.

Yeh has also been honored by the Golden Gate Chapter of American Women in Radio and Television for her outstanding achievements in broadcasting, the Asian Business League of San Francisco with a Distinguished Community Service award for serving as “a positive role model to the younger generation,” and the California State Attorney General with the Asian Pacific American Award.

In addition to all of her duties at KRON 4 and her many achievements outside the newsroom, Yeh has found time to participate in many community organizations.  She was a board member for the United Way and the Center for the Pacific Rim.  Yeh served on a steering committee and is now a board member for the Asian Pacific Fund.

A graduate of the University of Hawaii, Yeh majored in journalism and minored in political science.  In 1980, she received a Master’s degree in journalism from Columbia University.
