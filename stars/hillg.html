Gary Hill
KPTV co-anchor Gary Hill is a native son of Oregon and graduate of both Linfield College and the Northwestern school of law at Lewis and Clark College.

Hill grew up in Junction City, Oregon, where as a teenager he was an aspiring math teacher, until a friend talked him into working on the school newspaper.

"I got hooked on writing and reporting. I would have studied journalism in college except that Linfield, where I had a scholarship, didn't offer that degree," Hill said. "So I took all the writing I could and coupled it with political science.  Now I'm glad it worked out that way.  I have  a broader education and everything I've learned helps on a job like mine.

Hill came to KPTV in 1976 as political reporter and general assignment reporter before becoming co-anchor with Kim Singer in February of 1984. He has earned a law degree since joining KPTV and was admitted to the Oregon State Bar in 1982.

"It seemed like the natural thing to do," said Hill. "I'd been press secretary to Congressman Jim Weaver for one year, and my degree from Linfield College was in political science and communications. It all seemed to go together, along with writing and reporting the news."

Hill is married and he and his wife reside in Portland.  His hobbies are racquetball and cooking.

(KPTV Press release, May 22, 1990)
