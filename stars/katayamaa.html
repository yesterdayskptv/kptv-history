Anna Katayama

Anna Katayama joined the Oregon’s News 12 team as a reporter in April of 1995 and began co-anchoring the weekend 10 O’Clock News in December of that year.

She began her journalism career reporting, shooting and editing for a local cable station while attending San Francisco State University. After graduation she worked in the Bay Area on the assignment desk at KTVU-TV before accepting a reporting position with KNDO-TV, the NBC affiliate in Yakima, Washington.

From Yakima, she moved to Fort Smith, Arkansas where she co-anchored CBS affiliate KFSM-TV’s #1 rated 5:00 News and reported on stories that took her as far way as China. Since joining the Oregon’s News 12 team her work has been recognized by the Associated Press and she received a regional award in the Best of the West competition for a series on the gang problem in Portland.

Anna was born and raised in Okinawa, Japan. Her family now lives in Honolulu, Hawaii.
