Shauna Parsons

 

WHERE IS SHE NOW?
Shauna continues to anchor the weeknight edition of The 10 O'Clock News with Wayne Garcia, on the Meredith-owned KPTV Fox-12 Oregon. Parsons

After an exhaustive national search for a co-anchor for "The 10 O’Clock News" on KPTV-Oregon's 12, Shauna Parsons was named to the team. She debuted on "The 10 O’Clock News" alongside Kurt Ludlow on October 2nd, 2000.

She came to Portland from Salt Lake City, where she was morning anchor at KSTU. After years of getting up before the crack of dawn, Shauna is thrilled to finally be getting some sleep!

She's also thrilled to be doing her "Teens Making a Difference" segment on "The 10 O'Clock News," because it gives her the opportunity to get out into the community and meet some incredible young people.

"I think it's important to remind people that despite some of the bad news they read and hear about each day, there are a lot of very wonderful things happening in our community. We have a responsibility to bring our viewers the good news too," says Shauna.

Shauna has received awards from the Society of Professional Journalists, the Utah Broadcasters Association, and was nominated for an Emmy award for a story on animal euthanasia. One of Shauna’s most memorable stories was one in which she served both as photographer and reporter; she arranged to be checked into a battered women’s shelter where she documented the lives of the women and children who went there for help. On the lighter side, Shauna once passed out on camera while being "needled" by an acupuncturist for a story on alternative medicine.

Shauna started her journalism career in Provo, Utah while attending Brigham Young University. She anchored the nightly news on KBYU-TV, and produced and anchored a daily newscast on KBYU-FM. An internship at Salt Lake City Fox affiliate KSTU lead to an on-air reporting position, then subsequent jobs as weekend anchor, and morning anchor.

Shauna, her husband Ivan and her son Jackson are happy to call Portland home.
