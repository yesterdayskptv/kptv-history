Home team bios: Lori Matsukawa
http://www.king5.com/about/bios_matsukawa.html
Lori Matsukawa co-anchors KING 5 News at 10 p.m. weeknights on KONG TV 6/16. Lori was hired in 1983 as a reporter and co-anchor for "Top Story," a daily look at a major local story. She anchored KING 5 Morning News from 1988 to 1990, KING 5 Weekend News from 1990 to 1998 and KING 5 News at 11 from 1998 to1999. From 1984 to 1990, Lori also co-hosted "Celebrate the Differences," a weekly KING 5 minority affairs program.

 

Stories she has covered include the 2002 Winter Olympic Games in Salt Lake City, Gov. Gary Locke's first mission to China in 1997, a series of reports on Washington apples and businesses in Japan in 1995 and in 1991, she filed live reports for NBC affiliates during the 50th Anniversary of the attack on Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. Lori's personal favorite - she pulled 9-Gs in an F-16 in 1999.

Lori's professional awards include ARBY Awards in 1987, 1989, 1992, 1996 and 2000 given by the Academy of Religious Broadcasting; a Society of Professional Journalists award for Economic Reporting in 1989 and the "American Scene Award" from the local chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences in 1986.

As a community volunteer, Lori serves on the Association Board of the YMCA of Greater Seattle. In 1999, she organized the Student Broadcast Project for UNITY, a multi-cultural journalism convention held in Seattle. In 1996, she was chosen as an Asian-American Living Pioneer by the Northwest Asian Weekly Foundation and in 1993, she was given the Community Volunteer Award from the Asian Counseling and Referral Service, where she served as a board member and board president. She is a member of the Society of Professional Journalists and the Asian American Journalists Association (AAJA.) She is a founding member of AAJA's Seattle Chapter and served as Chapter president and co-chaired its National Convention in Seattle in 1991.

In 1974, Lori was crowned Miss Teenage America, offering her a chance to travel outside her native Hawaii - sparking her interest in journalism. She graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Stanford University with a B.A. in communication/journalism in 1978. She received an M.A. in communication from the University of Washington in 1996. Her previous work experience includes reporting, producing and/or anchoring at The Honolulu Advertiser, KRCR TV in Redding, Calif., KPTV in Portland, Ore., and KOMO-TV in Seattle, Wash.

Lori lives in Bellevue with her husband and son.
