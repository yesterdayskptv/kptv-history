Left to right: Patty Wasch (George's wife), Patty (Baldwin)

Thanks again!  --Ron Dunevant

A Note From Phil Cassidy to His KPTV Family

Hello to all my friends and extended family!

I just want to thank you all for your prayers and kind thoughts, and your food, while I've been working through my skin cancer battle these last 4 months. For some of you this might be new information. Don't worry. I'm feeling great.

I've been through two surgeries, and at this point, appear to be cancer free. What I have, has a 60 percent chance of coming back and so I will begin treatment by Wednesday of next week. This will be the hard part of what I need to go through to make sure it doesn't come back. It might last 4 months and it could take a year. I'm focusing on the 40 percent zero re-occurrence, 100 percent of the time. I feel like I have been blessed already with such good friends. Truthfully, you have all kept me positive and moving forward.

If you want to continue to support us in any way that would be great. Please, don't feel obligated. You all have lives, schedules and circles of your own that require attention. We will get 'err done. Please continue to pray, at least. God has already provided me with a few miracles along the way. A clear catscan and only one of 15 surgically removed lymph nodes proving to be cancerous, No doubt, he's at work, on my, and your behalf.

Please contact my wife Kris if you want to make contact. Please pray for Kris and the boys and ask for anxiety relief.

And please pray for several folks in this group e-mail who are going through cancer battles of their own.

Financially, we're going to take a bit of a hit and so I am going to work the video-transfer side of my video biz by offering folks a chance to transfer home videos to DVD at a reduced rate of $17 per DVD. I can make duplicates for an addition $6 each. If you've been putting off transferring videos to DVD please take advantage of this. I will be home all day and will have plenty of time and hopefully enough energy to make the transfers. Please label your tapes so I can label the DVD covers. Call me before you come over to make sure I'm up for it.  971-246-0260

Again, I'm feeling great. I will beat this. I already have mentally. I'm trying to laugh as much as possible and keep those good endorphins going.

But it all changes Wednesday.

You bet I'm having one last Margarita before the treatment begins.

With Love and Thanks,

Your Friend

Phil Cassidy

Monday January 23, 2006
Former KPTV News Director Joins CNN

JOHN SEARS, a veteran broadcaster with more than two decades of experience managing broadcast newsrooms in Florida and Oregon, joins CNN as the first bureau chief for the network's new Gulf Coast bureau in New Orleans, it was announced today by Jack Womack, senior vice president of domestic news operations and administration for CNN/U.S.

Sears comes to CNN from WOFL, Fox 35 in Orlando, Fla., where he recently served as vice president of news. During his tenure, the station's ratings for its key news programs showed consistent growth. Previously, Sears worked as a consultant for KDVR, Fox 31, in Denver, which hired him to help develop strategic editorial plans for its news department.

Sears also worked at KPTV in Portland, Ore., joining the station in 1986 as executive news producer. During that time, he earned a Gabriel Award for a documentary on a man who lost his legs in a train accident and supervised a Peabody Award-winning documentary about environmental and economic recovery 10 years after the eruption of Mount St. Helens. In 1991, he became the station's news director and spearheaded the launch of several successful programs, including a four-hour morning show, an investigative unit and a weekly news magazine.

Sears graduated cum laude with a bachelor of arts degree in communications from Brigham Young University and also completed pre-medical courses at Portland State University in Portland, Ore.

Saturday December 17, 2005
KPTV cancels "Good Day Lifestyles"
Jeff Gustin, Steve Beck and Gina Brasseur exit KPTV

Steve Beck and Jeff Gustin

Saturday, December 10, marked the final broadcast of KPTV's Good Day Lifestyles program.  The show, which debuted in September 2002 (shortly after the merger with KPDX) is to be replaced by a three-hour Saturday edition of Good Day Oregon in January.

The successor to KPTV's In the Garden with Mike Darcy, Lifestyles went through several different hosts and formats in its three-and-a-half seasons, but continued to show ratings growth.  It also appealed to a niche audience that is underserved by the Portland market, as the new KPTV shifts its focus away from special-interest programming in favor of news expansion.  Also lost with this decision: numerous advertisers who found success in the Lifestyles program, but do not buy "hard" news.

Former The 10 O'Clock News anchor Kim Singer, who  joined the program in October 2003, will continue her Better Homes and Gardens features, and will have some role in the new More Good Day Oregon program, which will air weekdays from 9:00 to 10:00.

Displaced by the move: long-time KPTV employees Jeff Gustin, Steve Beck and Gina Brausser.  Gina left KPTV a few weeks prior to take a job opportunity in Colorado Springs.  Best wishes and good luck to all of you!
